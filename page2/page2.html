Section2: Why it is better to use knife to sharpen pencils
Learning Objective: Benefits of using knifes & how to choose knifes

Although pencil sharpener is a convienient way, many artists choose to sharpen pencils by themselves to get their ideal profiles. The modern machines produce a single length and has no variation. Sometimes we need to lay the pencils more flat to do smooth shadows, so the inner cores of the pencils have to be exposed more. In this case, pencil sharpener is hard to complete the task. Moreover, pencil sharpener is limited to a fixed angle and you can't control how sharp your pencil tip is. Using a knife to sharpen pencils give you more control over the tip size based on your own requirement.

Further, some pencil sharpeners may easy to shatter the graphite within the pencils and make the tips breake. Then you are reuqired to do more sharpening, resulting in the pencils will get short quickly.

In addition to the benefits for the pencil itself, some artists think the process of sharpening pencils is an opportunity to take a break. Slowly taking the shavings off of a pencil, watching the pencil tip turn into a cone, and gradually forming the tip sharp you want, the process is completely under your control and just so satisfying. When we are concentrated on one thing (like drawing) for a long time, sometimes we become numb, and it is a good thing if we can switch our focus to another thing. Try it, maybe you will feel differently when you look back at your paintings.

Some recommendation for knife choice: a sharp blade, helping you make your desired shape of tip of pencils easily, and a sturdy handle, protecting you from slipping. There are many different types of knifes such as benchmade knifes, craft knifes, and razor blade...this is just a personal preference. At the beginning, a utility knife we use usually is a good choice.
