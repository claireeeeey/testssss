Section1: Let's first get to know our pencils
Learning Objective: HB Graphite Scale & Numerical Graphite Scale

You may realize that there is a combination of a number and a letter at the end the pencil bodies. This is the graphite grading scale. It is used to measure the hardness and darkness of the pencils.

Briefly, "H" indicates a hard pencil, and "B" stands for "black", indicating a softer lead. A softer pencil is easier to leave a darmer mark on the paper, and a hard pencil can keep the edge solid and precise. Your choice of the pencils should depend on the types of drawing.

The numerical scale classifies the degree of the pencil intensity. A larger number means that the pencil is harder/blacker.

There is a middle section of the pencil grade, including four types: B, HB, F, and H. They can be seen as the gradations of hardness and blackness. The term "F" stands for fine point and is widely used in daily life.
